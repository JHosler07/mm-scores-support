Privacy Policy

March Madness Live — Last updated: March 2026

Overview

March Madness Live ("the App") is a free sports scores application that displays NCAA Men's Basketball tournament scores, statistics, and broadcast information. Your privacy is important to us. This policy explains what data the App does and does not collect.

Data We Do Not Collect

The App does not collect, store, or transmit any personal information. Specifically, we do not collect:

  • Names, email addresses, or contact information
  • Location data
  • Device identifiers or advertising IDs
  • Usage analytics or tracking data
  • Photos, contacts, or any data from your device
  • Health, financial, or biometric data

The App does not require you to create an account or sign in.

Data the App Accesses

The App fetches publicly available game scores and schedule data from ESPN's public API. This data includes team names, scores, broadcast channels, and game times. This is public information that anyone can access through ESPN's website. No personal data is sent to ESPN as part of these requests.

Third-Party Services

The App does not integrate any third-party analytics, advertising, crash reporting, or tracking services. The only external service used is ESPN's publicly accessible scoreboard API for retrieving game data.

Data Storage

The App stores your preferences (such as dark mode and refresh rate settings) locally on your device only. This data is never transmitted to any server.

Children's Privacy

The App does not collect any personal information from anyone, including children under the age of 13. The App is safe for users of all ages.

Changes to This Policy

If we ever update this privacy policy, the changes will be posted on this page with an updated date. Since the App does not collect any data, we do not anticipate significant changes.
