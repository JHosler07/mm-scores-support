March Madness Live — Help & FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Why does the app say "No games found"?

Games are only available during the NCAA Tournament (mid-March through early April). Change the date to a tournament game day using the date selector at the top of the app.

Q: How often do scores update?

By default, scores refresh every 30 seconds. You can change this in Settings (gear icon) to 15 seconds, 1 minute, 2 minutes, or 5 minutes. You can also pull down on the game list to refresh manually.

Q: What is the Excitement Index?

A 0–100 score measuring how thrilling a game is. It considers score closeness, late-game tension, matchup quality (seed gap and spread), scoring pace, and lead changes. A close game between a 13-seed and a 4-seed scores higher than an 8 vs 9.

Q: What is the Upset Factor?

A 0–100 score measuring how likely an underdog win is. For live games, it uses projected margin of victory — if an underdog leads by 8 at the 10-minute mark, they're on pace to win by 32. It also factors in seed differential, elapsed time confidence, and spread defiance.

Q: What does the number in parentheses mean?

That's the point spread for the favored team. For example, (-6.5) means the favorite is expected to win by 6.5 points.

Q: How do I switch between dark and light mode?

Tap the sun/moon icon in the top-left corner of the app, or go to Settings (gear icon) and toggle Dark Mode.

Q: The app shows an error or won't load scores.

Check your internet connection and try pulling down to refresh. The app requires an active internet connection to fetch scores from ESPN.
